An Exploration of the Arxiv

1. What exactly is the Arxiv?

Arxiv is a site dedicated to providing free access to research papers to the public, and offering a platform for researchers to publish their work to a large audience. As part of that goal, they open source all data on the site, including all articles and their metadata. This is the dataset that supports all the works below. 2. Data Acquisition

The full metadata set can be found at either:

  • https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/Cornell-University/arxiv/versions/171/data
  • https://huggingface.co/datasets/arxiv_dataset

However, the full articles can be retrieved in a variety of ways and that information can also be found at the Kaggle links or the official Arxiv site: https://info.arxiv.org/help/bulk_data_s3.html

3. Data Overview

The metadata provided for each article is quite exhaustive but for our usage the relevant features in the data are:

  • Categories: The categories selected by the authors that the paper falls under. Can be broken into major and sub categories.
  • Abstract: The full abstract for the paper.
  • Versions: Data regarding publishing dates.

Let’s take a more quantitative look at that data though:

  • Total Articles Count: 2,440,876
  • Unique Categories: 155
  • Number of years: 18
  • Average Articles per year: 135,604
