An Exploration of the Arxiv

What exactly is the Arxiv?

Arxiv is a site dedicated to providing free access to research papers to the public, and offering a platform for researchers to publish their work to a large audience. As part of that goal, they open source all data on the site, including all articles and their metadata. This is the dataset that supports all the works below. 2. How has that landscape changes?

Let's roll the clock back to 2007 and look at the category distribution back then

As we can see physics was far and away the most published research subject; mathematics trailing in not a close second. Economics, Electrical Engineering, and Quantitative Finance were not supported topics yet. What you may find the most interesting though is computer science struggling to get 6%. Let's jump to now to 2023 and see how it's changed

Now in 2023 we can find a much more well rounded landscape, with computer science actually over taking physics for the top subject. Statstics has increased considerbly as well, and physics took an almost 50% hit in it's share.

3. Can we use AI to better understand and explore the underlying structure of the research?

Using all-mpnet-base-v2 we can project the texts of the abstracts (a randomized subset of 250k) into a dense embeddings space. Then with T-SNE we can further project these embeddings into 2 dimensions.

Potentially with a better backend model and dimensionality reduction algorithms we may be able to get more from this visualization but as of now the results are lackluster.
